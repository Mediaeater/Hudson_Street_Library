About the Library

Hudson Street Library houses a unique collection dedicated to documenting visual culture through the lens of contemporary photography. Our specialized focus areas include New York City, surveillance, female artists, street art, fashion, and music. We emphasize diverse global perspectives, featuring works from Middle Eastern and other underrepresented creators. Furthermore, the library boasts unique holdings such as rare zines, artist books, and special collections not commonly found elsewhere, alongside materials on books about books, LGBT+ publications, serial landscape photography, and photography of protest.

Publications

Discover publications produced by the Hudson Street Library, starting with our inaugural volume.

Surveillance Index Edition One

Our first publication explores the intersection of surveillance and photography through works from our collection. This compilation brings together critical essays alongside visual documentation examining how photography has both served surveillance and critiqued it.

Features include:

  • Historical context of surveillance photography
  • Contemporary artistic responses
  • Previously unpublished works
  • Critical essays by leading scholars
